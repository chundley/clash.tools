What is clash.tools?

Clash.tools (CT) is a web application built from the ground up to take the best features of other tools such as Clash Caller and Clash of Clans War Planner and combine them to create an elegant, fluid experience for managing clan wars.

Who is it for?

CT is for competitive war clans. It allows clan leaders to manage wars with a variety of flexible options. It also manages historical results providing a way to evaluate and improve members over time.

Who is it not for?

Casual war clans who aren't worried about security. For example, Clash Caller is great for casual war clans due to it's simplicity - but it's open nature means anyone can do anything, and there's no way to know who did what. This presents a significant problem when a less-than-trustworthy member decides to change all of your names and assignments for fun.

If your clan is opposed to creating member accounts to help manage wars and track statistics over time securly, clash.tools is not for you.

Why do you need my email address?

As with most hosted apps, your email address is used to keep track of your member account. In game names can be duplicated, and member tags are sometimes mis-read or mis-typed.

It also provides me a way to send new product feature announcements and in-app messages to you when appropriate.

Does everyone in my clan need a clash.tools account?

Although we don't require all clan members to have a clash.tools account, it is strongly recommended. Please read the detail on using placeholder values in your team's roster here.

Note that if a member doesn't sign up and you use the placeholder feature, none of their stats will be tracked and you'll have to use the placeholder feature every war.

I have two (or more) COC accounts. How do I get them all into clash.tools?

Each account in clash.tools requires a different email address. Whether your COC accounts are in different clans or not doesn't matter - you'll need a new email address for each CT account.

The easiest way to do this without driving yourself crazy with so many email addresses is use Gmail and the "fake alias" feature. Click here for Google's explanation.

For a real world example, let's say my email address is aaa@gmail.com. I have two COC accounts, one is minpin and one is billybob. I can create two clash.tools accounts with email addresses aaa@gmail.com and aaa+billy@gmail.com. Any email sent to either of these addresses goes to my normal aaa@gmail.com inbox. You can create as many of these as you want - any text after the '+' sign is basically ignored by Gmail.

What is Attack Value (AV)?

Attack value is a measure of how valuable each attack in a war is to the war effort. It's a complex topic and has it's own help article for those interested in understanding the calculation and theory behind it.
